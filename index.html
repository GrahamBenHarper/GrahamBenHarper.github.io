Publications

My publications have focused on weak Galerkin finite element methods for Darcy flow, Stokes flow, linear elasticity, and poroelasticity. These novel finite element methods redefine differential operators by taking the distributional sense of a derivative and then discretizing both of the \(H^1\) and \(L^2\) spaces. On any polygonal cell \( E \) in a mesh, a discrete weak function \( p \) is assigned one polynomial space \( P_{k_1} \) on the interior of \( E \) and one polynomial space \( P_{k_2} \) for each edge of \( E \). Gradients in the weak form of the PDE are replaced with a discrete weak gradient, denoted by \( \nabla_{w,d} \) (sometimes shortened to \( \nabla_w \)), which maps \( p \) into a space \( W_{\rm{grad}} \). The discrete weak gradient is defined through its action as the solution to a small linear system

$$ \int_E \nabla_{w,d}p \cdot \mathbf{w} = \int_{\partial E} p^\partial (\mathbf{w} \cdot \mathbf{n}) - \int_E p^\circ \nabla\cdot\mathbf{w}, \quad\quad \forall \mathbf{w} \in W_{\rm{grad}}. $$

To read more about these methods, I recommend reading some of the papers I've written.

Numerical Software

Computation and software development are integral to my research. All of my numerical computations are performed in Matlab or C++ using either the Darcy+, DarcyLite, or deal.II finite element libraries. I am also a contributor to each of these. I then visualize my numerical solutions using VisIt and ParaView.
